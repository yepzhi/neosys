ABSTRACT
Neosys Aeon is an open conceptual framework designed to improve the way individuals understand
reality and make evidence-based decisions. It is grounded in the diagnosis that the contemporary
abundance of information has not produced a proportional improvement in the quality of collective
thinking (Lewandowsky et al., 2017; Pennycook & Rand, 2019). The framework proposes integrating
applied epistemology, the scientific method, and responsible action into an open, revisable,
non-proprietary reference system, articulated through ten operational principles that progress
from the individual toward the collective.

Problem. The disconnect between empirical evidence and decision-making facilitates the spread of
pseudoscience and misinformation. Between 30% and 60% of adults in industrialized countries
struggle to distinguish scientific from pseudoscientific claims (OECD, 2016; Allum et al., 2008).

Proposal. Re-establish the chain understand → validate → apply as the basis for individual and
collective judgment, drawing on Popper's (1959) falsifiability, Kuhn's (1962) structures of
knowledge, and Paul & Elder's (2006) applied critical thinking.

CONTEXT AND DIAGNOSIS

1. Introduction

The scientific method remains, to this day, the most robust and falsifiable mechanism humanity
has developed to distinguish verified knowledge from mere opinion. Not because it is perfect,
but because it is built to detect and correct its own errors (Popper, 1959). Its strength lies
precisely in its revisability: unlike any dogmatic belief system, the scientific method does not
protect ideas — it puts them to the test.

Yet the mass availability of information has not produced a proportional improvement in the
quality of collective thinking. Research documents a paradox: access to more data can, in
certain contexts, reinforce pre-existing biases rather than correct them — a phenomenon known
as motivated reasoning (Kunda, 1990; Mercier & Sperber, 2017). Four systemic symptoms
are identified:

Informational overabundance without epistemic filters.
Content production grows exponentially while the frameworks to evaluate it remain static
(Floridi, 2014).

Adoption of unverified ideas.
Confirmation bias and availability heuristics lead people to accept claims that reinforce
prior beliefs (Kahneman, 2011).

Weakening of critical thinking.
Longitudinal studies show stagnation in formal reasoning skills among adults
(Arum & Roksa, 2011).

Confusion between opinion and knowledge.
The democratization of platforms equates in visibility anecdotal testimony with systematic
evidence (Lewandowsky et al., 2017).

Neosys Aeon emerges as a reference framework to correct this drift: aligning individual and
collective thinking with reality through verifiable evidence, without replacing science but
functioning as its integrating and applied framework. The full framework is available at
yepzhi.com/neosys.

3. Proposal: The Neosys Aeon Framework

The framework proposes a three-phase operational chain that integrates the traditions above into
a reference system applicable at individual and collective scale:

  UNDERSTAND — Epistemology: how do we know?
  VALIDATE — Scientific method: how do we verify?
  APPLY — Applied ethics: how do we act?

The framework is distinguished from other critical thinking systems by three structural
characteristics. Radical openness: it belongs to no entity and cannot be privatized; any
individual may adopt, modify, and distribute it without restriction, aligned with the principles
of open science (Nosek et al., 2015). Explicit revisability: its principles are starting points,
not immovable rules; the framework acknowledges its own fallibility and incorporates it as a
virtue, avoiding the self-contradiction that affects systems that declare themselves non-dogmatic
while formulating absolute principles. Results orientation: its value is not declarative but
functional, measured by its capacity to improve real decisions — drawing on Dewey's (1910)
pragmatism, which held that thought has value insofar as it transforms experience.

METHOD AND SCOPE

4. Science, Validation, and Knowledge

The scientific method is the most powerful tool for distinguishing what works from what we
believe works. Not because it is perfect, but because it incorporates systematic self-correction
mechanisms absent in any other belief-generating system (Sagan, 1996). Its operational cycle —
observation, hypothesis, experimentation, peer review, replication — sets standards that any
claim must meet before being incorporated into the accepted body of knowledge. Replicability, in
particular, constitutes a filter that no alternative knowledge system has managed to
institutionalize with equivalent rigor (Open Science Collaboration, 2015).

Four operational principles of valid knowledge:

1. Without systematic observation, there are no data.
   Anecdote does not equal evidence; personal experience is a starting point, not a conclusion.

2. Without falsifiability, there is no science.
   A claim that cannot in principle be refuted contributes no knowledge about reality
   (Popper, 1959).

3. Without replication, there is no reliable certainty.
   An unreproduced result is a pending hypothesis, not an established fact (Ioannidis, 2005).

4. Without application, knowledge is incomplete.
   Understanding that does not transform experience remains in the realm of speculation
   (Dewey, 1910).

SCOPE AND LIMITS

6. Open Nature

Neosys Aeon is an open system in both the technical and philosophical sense. It requires no
affiliation, registration, or institutional validation to be adopted; it cannot be owned by any
entity. This reflects the conviction that frameworks of thought, like knowledge itself, gain
value through distribution, not concentration. The open science model — documented by Nosek
et al. (2015) and the Budapest Open Access Initiative (2002) — demonstrates that shared
knowledge is validated, improved, and extended more efficiently than restricted knowledge.

7. Distinction and Scope

Five precise delimitations define what Neosys Aeon is not:

It is not a religion.
It proposes no supernatural entities and requires no faith as a condition. Every claim within
the framework must, in principle, be falsifiable.

It is not a closed belief system.
Its principles are starting points, not decrees. They can — and should — be challenged with
evidence. A framework that does not admit revision contradicts its own premise.

It is not an organization.
It has no hierarchy, central leadership, or formal membership. Its adoption is individual
and voluntary.

It does not replace science.
It functions as an integrating framework: it helps interpret and apply the products of
science, not generate them.

It is not a product.
It has no commercial purpose. There are no economic access barriers or restrictive conditions
of use. The complete framework is available at yepzhi.com/neosys.

8. Framework Review Criteria

A framework that demands falsifiability from every system of ideas must apply that same demand
to itself. This section defines the conditions under which Neosys Aeon should be considered
insufficient, corrected, or replaced.

Signs of failure in individual application. The framework is not being used correctly when an
adopter employs its principles to justify decisions already made rather than to evaluate them;
when evidence is cited selectively to confirm prior beliefs — motivated reasoning disguised as
method —; or when the language of the framework is adopted as a marker of identity without any
actual change in real decisions. The criterion of validation is behavioral, not declarative.

Signs of failure as a system. Neosys Aeon has failed as an epistemic tool if two people who
apply it consistently to the same problem with the same available evidence cannot reduce their
disagreement in a structured way; if its principles prove so general that no claim can be
ruled out; or if a future version cannot contradict an earlier one with evidence. The inability
to produce legitimate disagreement among its users is a sign of emptiness, not consensus.

Condition of obsolescence. This framework must be substantially revised or replaced if, after
sustained application, it cannot be demonstrated that its users make decisions more coherent
with available evidence than those who do not use it. Its value is functional, not symbolic.
A framework that does not improve real judgment does not fulfill its purpose.

Governance and review process. Official versions of the framework are published by the founding
author at yepzhi.com/neosys. Anyone may propose corrections, flag inconsistencies, or suggest
improvements through the contact form on the site. Proposals are evaluated on the basis of their
evidential support, not the authority of whoever submits them. As the community of practice
grows, a Review Committee is planned, composed of researchers holding doctoral degrees and with
active records in scientific research, whose role will be to evaluate and validate updates to
the framework with the rigor the framework itself demands. Until then, editorial responsibility
rests with the founding author, who publicly assumes that role and may be challenged by any
adopter of the framework.

9. Conclusion

Progress — individual and collective — depends on the capacity to understand reality and act
on evidence. Comprehension without validation generates error. Action without evidence generates
unpredictable consequences. Evidence without application remains inert.

Neosys Aeon does not claim to have all the answers. It aims to ask better questions — and to
take seriously what the evidence returns. In an environment characterized by informational
overload and the weakening of institutional critical thinking, a shared, open, and revisable
reference framework is not an intellectual luxury: it is a practical necessity. Its value is
not measured by how many adopt it, but by how many use it to make better decisions, build with
greater rigor, and transmit what they have learned.
